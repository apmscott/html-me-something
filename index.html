Mount Rainier, Summer 2019

In summer 2019, I was a seasonal employee at Mount Rainier National Park (MORA).
Since living on a mountain isn't something I do everyday, I made a point of capturing her many faces and moods.

On Arrival

I lived in the basement of a house at Longmire, and frequently walked to a spot nearby on the Nisqually River to get photos
from that same location thoughout my stay.

June 8, 8:15pm, from Paradise

Evenings at the River

On many evenings, I would cross the historic wood suspension bridge and wander along the Nisqually River. It was the
perfect place to find fossils, see wildlife, and watch the colors of sunset bathe the mountain.

June 13, 8:53pm, from Nisqually at Longmire

Clouds Do Funky Stuff

It is said that the mountain makes its own weather. At 14,410 feet, is it easy to see how this can be the case. Lenticular clouds,
which form "hats" or "caps" over the mountain indicate that precipitation is on its way - at least in the upper elevations.

July 5, 12:23pm, from Nisqually at Longmire

A View from the East

My projects provided many opportunities for exploring the vast wilderness, including a 3-day backpacking trip on a section of the
Wonderland Trail in the eastern portion of the park. The peak wasn't always visible from the trail due to the layers of other towering ridges
and outcroppings, but when it was unmistakable when in view.

July 25, 9:13 am, on the Wonderland Trail near the head of Nickel Creek

On the Mountain

Another opoprtunity: hiking to Camp Muir, the stopping point before summiting! Starting from Paradise, we hiked to Camp Muir,
a collection of rock and wood shelters for rangers, guides, and climbers, barely visible in the background beyond the hikers.
A new highest elevation for me: 10,110 ft.

July 29, 9:13 am, on the trail to Camp Muir on Mt. Rainier

Bushwacking from the West

A last-minute project came up late in my season, which required us to access an area along the Wonderland Trail on the west side
of the park. We only had enough time for an overnight, so we cut through the forest from lumber company property.

August 6, 11:40 am, at the park border on the bushwhack to the Wonderland Trail

To the End, My Friend

As my season neared the end, the weather became less cooperative. Rain was common and the Mountain was generally more socked in.
Was she pouting because I was leaving? Perhaps. I know I was. But, I'll be back someday...

August 16, 7:09 pm, from Nisqually at Longmire
